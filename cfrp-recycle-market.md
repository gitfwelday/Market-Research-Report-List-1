<p><ul><li>Chemical Process</li><li>Physical Process</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The CFRP (Carbon Fiber Reinforced Polymer) recycling market comprises two primary types: chemical and physical processes. </p><p>Chemical processes involve breaking down CFRP into its original components through solvents or thermal methods, allowing for the recovery of carbon fibers and resins for reuse in new products. </p><p>Physical processes focus on mechanically grinding or shredding CFRP to create recycled materials. These materials can be utilized in various applications, such as construction or automotive components, providing an environmentally friendly alternative while reducing waste.</p></p>
